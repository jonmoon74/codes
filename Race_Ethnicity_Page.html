Support resources on Race and Ethnicity

Following the tragic and untimely death of a young black man in Minnesota I wanted to share some thoughts and words with you during what is certainly a pivotal time for us as a generation.

Personally, I am more than aware that as a white person I have privileges and I am choosing to use those privileges to ensure that I actively stand with the opposed. I recognise I do not know your struggles first-hand, I do not understand, however I stand.

I am indeed proud to work for a company that holds diversity and inclusion at the heart of its core values, however I am aware there is still much to be done.

Change will not come if we wait for some other person or some other time, we are the ones we’ve been waiting for and we are the change that we seek. We must take the time to continue to educate ourselves on how we can move forward together. Together as one.

I will continuously contribute in any way I can to ensure that people of colour in our business and all around me feel the safety, inclusion and value that is deserved; you are valued, you are talent, you are no lesser than any other and should never be made to feel this way.

Rachel Jones, Director of Talent, Learning, Engagement, Wellbeing and Organisational Design UK & Ireland
